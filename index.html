About me

Learn more about me, my work and my interests.

Jason Street
Front End developer

My name is Jason Street. I am a front end developer. My main areas of knowledge are in HTML, CSS and Javascript.

I am a front end designer/developer, experienced in HTML, CSS and Javascript. I also have a good working knowledge in other areas of computing, such as C# and Databases.

At secondary school I studied many different subjects, which I have been able to transfer to my working styles. Even before going into a career in development, I enjoyed IT and computingand while at 6th form and Secondary school, I participated in IT related projects and I enjoyed problem solving, as well as helping others with IT issues.

After finishing 6th Form I completed an apprenticeship in Business Administration, but I knew after earning my degree, I wanted to get back into IT and development. I looked into programming and I began to teach myself basic programming languages, before paying to enroll in a Just IT course and pursuing a career in development full time.

While at Just IT, I studied front-end languages, including Javascript, HTML and CSS. I knew that I wanted to pursue a career in front end development, however learning about back end technologies was interesting the course has opened my eyes to new things.

My coding timeline

  • 2009

    Hello World

    The first ever piece of code I wrote was the standard "Hello, World!" programme, in visual basic. I wrote this in roughly 2009 along with some other code, but it would be a few years later that I would pursue it as a career.

  • 2014-2016

    Early Steps

    In 2015 I created my first website. At that time I created it to host pictures for my personal and college work. I used a basic template and tools to create it and it gave me an insight into web design and I knew it was something I wanted to pursue.


    During my apprenticeship I also created a new website for the company I worked for. This was based on a similar design to my own personal website. I created the website myself, and I also desined the menus, layout and I took many of the pictures on display on the website. You can view the website here.

  • 2017

    Transition to full time development & Just IT

    Or, how I learned to stop worrying and love to code.

    I enrolled in the Just IT Class of September 2017, in the full time developers course. During this time I developed my knowledge in back-end and front-end development, and completed exams to prove my knowledge in these areas.


    One of the most important things I learned was that, as developers, we didn't need to know absolutely everything about programming languages. I realised that, being a developer isn't so much about knowing everything, but rather, knowing what is required and where to look, or who to ask.

  • 2018-2019

    Code Institute

    In 2018 after months of hard work, I got a job working as a developer at The Learning People. Here I am able to further develop my skills in back-end and front-end programming.


    At The Learning People I was also offered a chance to enrole with Code Institute in their full stack development course. While on the course I was able to create multiple projects, including my portfolio and my smaller projects.
